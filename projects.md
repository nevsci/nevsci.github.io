## 2026 — Custom NMR Tube Rack for Glovebox Work

During routine air-sensitive sample preparation, I needed a compact rack for handling NMR tubes inside the glovebox. Commercial options were either too large, unstable, or absurdly overpriced.

I designed a small low-profile holder with:
- 10 tube positions
- wider base for stability
- solvent-resistant geometry
- easy one-hand handling with gloves

After several ugly prototypes and one deeply embarrassing failed print, the final version worked surprisingly well and is now permanently living next to the Schlenk line.

## 2025 — Machine Learning for Spiropyran Photoswitch Design

As part of our work on spiropyran photoswitches, we explored how machine learning could help prioritize promising candidates before synthesis.

Instead of relying purely on intuition (or caffeine concentration), we combined computational descriptors with literature data to identify structural patterns connected to switching efficiency and fatigue resistance.

The project was particularly interesting because it sat exactly at the interface between chemistry and data science—where both fields believe the other one is doing magic.

This work contributed to the publication:

**Machine learning driven design of spiropyran photoswitches**  
Digital Discovery, 2025

## 2024 — Simple Solvent Bottle Label System

An unnecessarily annoying problem in many labs is that solvent bottles slowly evolve into anonymous transparent liquids with increasingly philosophical labels.

To reduce this, I created a simple printable label system:
- large visible hazard category
- refill tracking date
- owner / project field
- QR code linking to SDS

Nothing revolutionary, but surprisingly effective.

The labels were designed for quick printing on standard adhesive sheets and have survived several months of aggressive solvent exposure and human negligence.
